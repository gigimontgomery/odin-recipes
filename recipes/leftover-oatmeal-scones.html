Leftvoer Oatmeal Scones

A perfect winter snack

Every weekday morning in the winter I make oatmeal for my kids. They eat it blindly as they wake up and it's a warm to the day, but how much I make and they eat varies since I am eyeballing everything with groggy eyes.

With the appetites of these teens and a preteen growing constantly, I decided to start turning these into scones. Something I did when they were little and completely forgot about. Warm with a drizzle of honey, these are a nice addition to their lunch or afters school snack.

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 2 tbsp honey
  • 1 cup cooked oatmeal
  • 2/3 cup milk
  • 1 cup unbleached flour
  • 3/4 cup whole wheat flour
  • 1/2 tsp cinnamon
  • 4 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp salt

Steps

  1. Melt the butter and honey together, and pour them over the cool, cooked oatmeal. Add in the milk and whisk this mixture together until it's evenly combined.
  2. Mix the dry ingredients. You may want to adjust the salt depending on how salty your oatmeal was.
  3. Combine the two mixtures with a wooden spoon. If it seems too moist, add a little more flour. You want the dough to form a rough ball.
  4. Scoop it up in your hands and gently pat into a nice circle about an inch thick on a cookie sheet well greased with butter. If the dough still seems really sticky, dust it with some more flour until you can pat the surface down smooth. Cut into 8 wedges.
  5. Bake at 350 degrees Fahrenheit for 20-25 minutes, or until nicely browned on top (but not too dark). You need to enjoy these while still warm, so the butter will melt.
